# Welcome to Vibe Check

Vibe Check is a Claude Code plugin that tracks your coding session and gently reminds you to take care of your body. No OS popups or interruptions — reminders appear as visually distinct cards right in your Claude conversation.

```
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
⏰ VIBE CHECK — 👁️ Eye Reset (20 min since last break)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Look at something 20+ feet away for 20 seconds (the 20-20-20 rule). Then blink
rapidly 15 times to re-lubricate your eyes.
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### Intelligent Break Detection

- **Break compliance tracking** — measures whether you actually took the suggested break based on the gap before your next prompt
- **Spontaneous break detection** — if you step away for 15+ minutes without a pending reminder, all timers reset automatically
- **Multi-session support** — multiple Claude Code sessions share the same timers automatically
- **Seamless continuation** — if an active session already exists (within the last 60 min), new sessions join without resetting timers

## Evidence-Based Science

The timing intervals in Vibe Check are rooted in scientific research:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="20-20-20 Rule for Eye Health">
    Endorsed by the American Academy of Ophthalmology to reduce digital eye strain. Every 20 minutes, look at something 20+ feet away for 20 seconds.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="52-17 Work-Break Ratio">
    The DeskTime study found top performers work ~52 minutes then break ~17 minutes, aligning with natural ultradian rhythms. Vibe Check's 50-minute interval follows this evidence.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Hydration and Cognition">
    Even 1-2% dehydration impairs cognitive performance by up to 25% (Journal of Nutrition, 2012). Regular hydration reminders keep your brain functioning optimally.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Micro-breaks Reduce Discomfort">
    A meta-analysis in PLOS ONE found brief breaks reduce physical discomfort and improve task performance across occupations.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

- Eye exercises (20-20-20, palming, near-far focus)
- Breathing techniques (box breathing, 4-7-8, physiological sigh)
- Ergonomic checklist (monitor, chair, posture)
